#### Ingredients (for 3 persons)

**For the Jerusalem artichoke soup**:
- 600 g topinambours (Jerusalem artichokes), peeled and chopped
- 1 medium potato (about 150 g), peeled and chopped
- 1 small onion, chopped
- 15 mL olive oil
- ½ vegetable bouillon cube
- 600 mL water
- 100 mL crème fraîche or heavy cream
- Salt and pepper to taste
- Optional: drizzle of truffle oil or crushed walnuts for garnis

**For the rustic country bread**:
- 6 slices of rustic country bread (pain de campagne)
- 1 garlic clove, halved
- Olive oil or butter for brushing

**For the poached egg**:
- 3 fresh eggs
- 1 tbsp white vinegar
- Water for poaching

### Instructions

- In a pot, **heat** olive oil and sauté the onion until translucent.
- **Add** chopped topinambours and potato. Stir for 2–3 minutes.
- **Pour in** water and add the bouillon cube. Simmer for 25–30 minutes until vegetables are tender.
- **Blend** until smooth. Add cream, season with salt and pepper, and reheat gently.
- **Serve** hot, optionally garnished with a swirl of truffle oil or crushed walnuts
- Lightly **brush** bread slices with olive oil or butter.
- **Toast** in a pan or oven until golden and crisp.
- **Rub** each slice with the cut side of garlic while still warm.
- **Bring** a pot of water to a gentle simmer (not boiling). Add vinegar.
- **Crack** each egg into a small bowl.
- **Stir** the water to create a gentle whirlpool, then slide in the egg.
- **Poach** for 3 minutes until the white is set but yolk remains runny.
- **Remove** with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towel.
